Aurora recently began learning how to knit a top-down raglan cardigan using I Love This Cotton! yarn from Hobby Lobby. This project has been both a learning experience and a creative challenge, as she has been practicing new techniques such as casting on for a seamless yoke, increasing along raglan lines, and managing multiple stitch markers. Working from the neckline down allows her to try on the cardigan as she goes, helping her to understand shaping and fit in real time. The soft, breathable cotton yarn makes it enjoyable to handle and perfect for creating a comfortable, everyday garment.

Aurora recently learned how to use the Toybox Alpha 2 3D printer, exploring how to search for, download, and prepare designs for printing. It was exciting to see digital models come to life as she experimented with different settings and watched the printer layer each piece with precision. One of her favorite projects was creating an articulated Rocktopus—a flexible octopus toy—for her middle schooler. Seeing their excitement as the colorful, movable creature took shape made the learning process even more rewarding and inspired her to explore more 3D printing possibilities.

Aurora has always loved reading and exploring different worlds through books. Some of her favorites include Emma by Jane Austen, Coraline by Neil Gaiman, and the Harry Potter series. Currently, she's reading The Let Them Theory by Mel Robbins. It's a a short but powerful book about letting go of the need to control others’ opinions and actions, and instead focusing on peace, self-awareness, and acceptance. It gives her a lot to think about, so she reads it in small amounts to really absorb the message and reflect on how it applies to her own life. Her Goodreads account helps keep track of her reading journey, including books she's currently reading, those she's finished, and her ever-growing to-be-read list.
